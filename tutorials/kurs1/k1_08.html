K1.8a: Translation

Move the Drawing Plane

  • With the "Translate" block from the "p5-Helper" category, the drawing plane can be moved. When the drawing plane is moved, the coordinate origin is also moved.

    A circle drawn at point (0/0) does not change its coordinates through the "Translate" block, because the entire coordinate system is moved.

    All figures drawn after the "Translate" block are moved exactly the same. Figures drawn before the "Translate" block remain unchanged at their position.

  • With the following program you can observe the behavior of figures during translation.

    Note: The mouse coordinates are always given relative to the upper left corner, even when the drawing area is translated.

 K1.8b: Rotation

Rotate Around the Origin

  • With the "Rotate" block from the "p5-Helper" category, the drawing plane can be rotated. The drawing plane is always rotated around the coordinate origin at point P(0/0).

    If you specify the rotation angle with a positive number, the rotation occurs clockwise.

Rotate Around a Point on the Drawing Area

  • If a figure should be rotated around a point on the drawing plane, this can be achieved by combining a translation and a rotation.

    First, you move the coordinate system to the point around which you want to rotate, and then rotate the drawing plane by the desired angle.

 K1.8c: Animated Rotation

Variable as Angle

  • If a figure should rotate animatedly without being controlled by the mouse, this can be achieved with the help of a variable whose value changes.

  • On each pass through the "draw" block, the variable "angle" is increased by 2, so that the rectangle rotates a bit further in each frame. This creates the impression of an animation.

 K1.8d: Different Rotation and Translation

push - pop

  • If some figures should be rotated and translated differently than others, you can use the "push - pop" block from the "p5-Helper" category to limit the effect of blocks.

    In the following example, the white circle and cross should remain unchanged at their position, while the yellow circle should rotate.

  • The black lines and white circle are first translated in the "push-pop" block and then the color is set. These blocks only affect the figures that are also in the same "push-pop" block.

    The two yellow circles are in a different "push-pop" block and rotate around the coordinate origin, which was moved to the center of the drawing area. Since the small circle sits exactly on the rotation center, you don't notice its rotation.
