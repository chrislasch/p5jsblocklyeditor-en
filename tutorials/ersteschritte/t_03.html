Tutorial: Working with Blocks

Start Example Program:
Add a Comment

  • A block can be supplemented with a comment. A comment is an informational text and is not executed.

    Click with the right mouse button on a block and then click with the left mouse button on "Add Comment".

    Click on the "Question mark" symbol and type a comment.

    A comment can be hidden and shown by repeatedly clicking on the "Question mark" symbol.

Move a Block

  • If you click on a block and drag it with the left mouse button pressed, this block and all blocks below it will be moved.

  • To move only a single block, click with the left mouse button on the block to select it and then press the "Ctrl" and "x" keys simultaneously. The block will be cut.

    To paste the block back, press the "Ctrl" and "v" keys simultaneously. The block appears again in the editor and you can drag it to the desired position.

Delete a Block

  • To delete a block, there are four options:

    1) Click on a block with the left mouse button and then press the "Ctrl" and "x" keys simultaneously.

    2) Click on a block with the left mouse button and then press the "Delete" key.

    3) Click on a block with the right mouse button and then click "Delete x blocks".

    4) Click on a block and drag this block and all following ones to the toolbar or trash.

Restore a Block

  • All deleted blocks are temporarily stored in the trash.

    To restore a deleted block, click on the trash symbol so that the trash contents are displayed. In the trash, click on a block which is then copied to the editor.

Copy a Block

  • To copy a block, there are two options:

    1) Click on a block with the left mouse button and then press the "Ctrl" and "c" keys simultaneously. Then press the "Ctrl" and "v" keys simultaneously and drag the block to the desired position.

    2) Click on a block with the right mouse button, then click "Copy" and drag the inserted block to the desired position.

Undo an Action

  • You can undo the last actions by clicking with the right mouse button on an empty editor area and then clicking with the left mouse button on "Undo".
